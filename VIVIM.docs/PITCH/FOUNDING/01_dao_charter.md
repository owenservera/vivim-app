# VIVIM DAO Charter

## The Constitution of the VIVIM Decentralized Autonomous Organization

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** [TO BE DETERMINED]  
**Status:** Founding Document

---

## PREAMBLE

We, the founding members of VIVIM, hereby establish this Charter to govern our collective endeavor in building the infrastructure for user-sovereign AI knowledge management.

Recognizing that:
- Users should own their AI conversations and the knowledge extracted therefrom
- Decentralized governance enables transparency, fairness, and resilience
- Technology should empower individuals rather than control them

We commit to operating VIVIM as a fully transparent, DAO-governed organization where decisions are made democratically, contributions are rewarded equitably, and the community holds ultimate authority.

---

## ARTICLE I: NAME AND PRINCIPAL OFFICE

### Section 1.1 - Name
The name of this organization shall be "VIVIM DAO" (the "DAO").

### Section 1.2 - Principal Office
The DAO shall maintain its principal office at locations determined by the Governance Committee. The DAO operates as a distributed organization with no fixed physical location, in accordance with the virtual-first principle.

### Section 1.3 - Registered Agent
The DAO shall designate a registered agent for legal notification purposes, as required by applicable jurisdictions.

---

## ARTICLE II: PURPOSE AND MISSION

### Section 2.1 - Mission
VIVIM's mission is to democratize access to AI-generated knowledge while ensuring privacy, portability, and user control.

### Section 2.2 - Core Objectives
1. **User Sovereignty**: Enable users to own their data, keys, and identity
2. **Platform Agnosticism**: Support interoperability across AI providers
3. **Privacy First**: Implement zero-knowledge architecture with end-to-end encryption
4. **Decentralization**: Ensure no central authority controls knowledge access
5. **Semantic Understanding**: Transform conversations into searchable, linkable knowledge

### Section 2.3 - Public Benefit
The DAO operates for the public benefit by promoting:
- Digital sovereignty and privacy rights
- Open standards for AI data portability
- Accessible AI tools for all users regardless of technical ability

## ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### Section 3.1 - Governance Bodies

The DAO shall be governed by the following bodies, in order of authority:

1. **Token Holder Assembly** (Highest Authority)
2. **Governance Committee** (Executive Administration)
3. **Treasury Committee** (Financial Oversight)
4. **Technical Committee** (Technical Decisions)
5. **Community Moderation Council** (Community Health)

### Section 3.2 - Token Holder Assembly

#### Composition
All holders of VIVIM Governance Tokens ("VGT") shall be members of the Token Holder Assembly.

#### Powers
The Token Holder Assembly shall have the following exclusive powers:
- Amend this Charter
- Approve major protocol changes
- Ratify the annual budget
- Elect members to Governance Committee
- Approve treasury expenditures above threshold
- Remove committee members for cause
- Dissolve the DAO or merge with another entity

#### Voting Rights
Each VGT holder shall have voting rights proportional to their token holdings, subject to the following principles:
- One-token, one-vote for routine proposals
- Quadratic voting may be implemented for certain proposal types to prevent plutocracy
- Community contributors may receive voting power boosts through reputation

#### Meeting Frequency
- **Regular Assemblies**: Quarterly, with at least 14 days notice
- **Special Assemblies**: May be called by Governance Committee or 10% of voting power

### Section 3.3 - Governance Committee

#### Composition
The Governance Committee shall consist of 5 members:
- 3 elected by Token Holder Assembly
- 1 appointed by founding team (permanent, non-voting advisory role)
- 1 rotating community representative

#### Qualifications
Committee members must:
- Hold at least 1,000 VGT
- Have been active in the DAO for at least 90 days
- Not be a citizen of sanctioned jurisdictions

#### Terms
- Standard term: 12 months
- Staggered elections: 1-2 seats up for election each quarter
- Maximum consecutive terms: 3

#### Powers
The Governance Committee shall:
- Execute decisions approved by Token Holder Assembly
- Manage day-to-day DAO operations
- Approve expenditures below threshold
- Propose policy changes
- Call regular and special assemblies
- Manage service provider relationships

### Section 3.4 - Treasury Committee

#### Composition
3 members, elected by Token Holder Assembly

#### Powers
- Monitor treasury health and sustainability
- Propose budget allocations
- Review large expenditures
- Report quarterly on financial status
- Recommend treasury diversification strategies

### Section 3.5 - Technical Committee

#### Composition
Lead developers and technical contributors, minimum 3 members

#### Powers
- Make technical decisions within approved parameters
- Approve protocol upgrades
- Maintain code quality standards
- Report on technical roadmap progress

### Section 3.6 - Community Moderation Council

#### Composition
5 community members elected by reputation-weighted vote

#### Powers
- Moderate community spaces
- Enforce community guidelines
- Resolve disputes
- Recommend community program changes

## ARTICLE IV: PROPOSALS AND VOTING

### Section 4.1 - Proposal Types

| Type | Description | Quorum | Passing Threshold |
|------|-------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Constitutional Amendment** | Changes to this Charter | 30% | 66% |
| **Major Protocol** | Significant technical changes | 20% | 60% |
| **Minor Protocol** | Technical improvements | 10% | 50% |
| **Budget** | Treasury allocations | 15% | 55% |
| **Community** | Programs, grants, guidelines | 5% | 50% |
| **Emergency** | Critical security matters | 50% | 75% |

### Section 4.2 - Proposal Process

1. **Drafting**: Any member may draft a proposal
2. **Discussion**: Minimum 7-day discussion period
3. **Amendment**: Proposals may be amended during discussion
4. **Voting**: 5-day voting window
5. **Execution**: Results implemented within 14 days of passage
6. **Review**: Post-implementation review at next assembly

### Section 4.3 - Voting Mechanisms

#### Off-Chain Voting
- Forum-based discussion and signal voting
- Used for non-binding resolutions and governance direction

#### On-Chain Voting
- Smart contract-based voting
- Used for binding decisions and token-weighted votes

#### Delegated Voting
- Token holders may delegate voting power to representatives
- Delegation can be revoked at any time

### Section 4.4 - Emergency Powers

In case of immediate threat to the DAO:
- Technical Committee may enact emergency fixes
- Governance Committee may pause contract functions
- Emergency proposals require 75% approval with 50% quorum

## ARTICLE V: TREASURY AND ECONOMICS

### Section 5.1 - Treasury Holdings

The DAO Treasury shall hold:
- Protocol revenue (premium features, API access)
- Community contributions
- Investment returns
- Token reserves

### Section 5.2 - Budget Cycle

- **Annual Budget**: Approved by Token Holder Assembly
- **Quarterly Reviews**: Treasury Committee reports
- **Emergency Funds**: Up to 5% of treasury may be allocated without prior approval

### Section 5.3 - Expenditure Thresholds

| Amount | Approval Required |
|--------|-------------------|
| < €5,000 | Single Committee Member |
| €5,000 - €25,000 | Governance Committee Vote |
| €25,000 - €100,000 | Token Holder Assembly |
| > €100,000 | Super-Majority (66%) Assembly |

### Section 5.4 - Dividend Distribution

The DAO may distribute dividends to users as follows:
- Revenue sharing shall be determined by proposal
- Dividend pools shall be calculated based on contribution metrics
- Minimum 50% of net revenue may be allocated to user dividends upon proposal passage

## ARTICLE VI: RIGHTS AND DUTIES OF MEMBERS

### Section 6.1 - Token Holder Rights

Every VGT holder shall have the right to:
- Vote on proposals
- Propose legislation
- Participate in governance discussions
- AccessDAO records and financial reports
- Request inclusion on governance agenda
- Appeal decisions through dispute resolution

### Section 6.2 - Member Duties

Members shall:
- Act in good faith
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Respect community guidelines
- Participate in governance when able
- Maintain security of tokens and keys

### Section 6.3 - Reputation System

The DAO shall maintain a reputation system that:
- Rewards valuable contributions
- Provides voting power boosts for consistent participation
- Tracks milestone completion and bounty work
- Enables community leadership recognition

---

## ARTICLE VII: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### Section 7.1 - Internal Resolution

1. **Good Faith Negotiation**: Parties shall attempt to resolve disputes directly
2. **Mediation**: Community Moderation Council may mediate disputes
3. **Arbitration**: Binding arbitration by selected DAO arbitrators

### Section 7.2 - External Resolution

For disputes not resolvable internally:
- DAO shall designate arbitration under [Jurisdiction to be determined]
- Arbitration shall follow DAO arbitration rules
- Decisions are final and binding

---

## ARTICLE VIII: AMENDMENTS

### Section 8.1 - Amendment Process

This Charter may be amended by:
1. Proposal by any member with 100+ VGT or 30+ days active reputation
2. 14-day discussion period
3. 7-day voting period
4. 66% approval with 30% quorum

### Section 8.2 - Fundamental Principles

The following principles may not be amended:
- User sovereignty over data
- Transparency of governance
- Equal voting rights for token holders
- Open participation

---

## ARTICLE IX: WINDING DOWN

### Section 9.1 - Dissolution

The DAO may be dissolved by:
- Super-majority (75%) approval of Token Holder Assembly
- Complete distribution of assets to token holders
- Publication of final audit report

### Section 9.2 - Asset Distribution

Upon dissolution:
- Treasury assets liquidated
- Creditors paid
- Remaining assets distributed proportionally to token holders
- All data and IP released as open source
